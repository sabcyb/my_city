Frankfurt am Main

Three must see places in Frankfurt am Main

Römerberg

Römerberg, the historic heart of Frankfurt am Main, is a picturesque square where medieval charm meets modern urban life. Lined with reconstructed half-timbered houses, the square evokes centuries of German history while serving as a vibrant focal point for residents and visitors. Dominated by the Römer, a complex of gabled townhouses that has housed the city hall for over six centuries, the plaza is also overlooked by the Gothic Nikolaikirche and the Old St. Nicholas Church, adding architectural depth. Market stalls and seasonal events animate the cobblestones throughout the year: a traditional weekly market, open-air concerts, and the world-famous Christmas market transform the space into a lively social hub. Cafés and restaurants spilling onto terraces invite leisurely observation of everyday life, while statues and fountains provide photographic highlights. Despite wartime destruction and extensive rebuilding after World War II, careful restoration has preserved the square’s historic silhouette, blending authenticity with contemporary amenities. Römerberg functions as both a gateway to Frankfurt’s museums and riverfront and as a compact snapshot of the city’s resilience, cultural richness, and communal spirit. For anyone visiting Frankfurt, it remains an essential place to experience the city’s past and present in one intimate, memorable setting and timeless charm.

Alte Oper Haus

Alte Oper is Frankfurt’s historic concert hall and former opera house, celebrated for its richly ornamented neo-Renaissance façade and grand presence on Opernplatz. Opened in 1880 as the city’s cultural flagship, the building became a symbol of civic pride and musical life until severe wartime damage in World War II left it largely destroyed. A long postwar civic campaign led to meticulous restoration and a triumphant reopening in 1981; today the Alte Oper functions primarily as a concert and event venue rather than an opera house. Its lavish marble foyers, sweeping staircases and ornate halls host an eclectic program spanning symphonic concerts, chamber music, jazz, pop and large-scale gala events. Acoustics and atmosphere combine with architectural splendor to create a memorable audience experience, while the plaza outside — often animated by street life, cafés and seasonal markets — makes the site a social as well as cultural focal point. Located near Frankfurt’s shopping boulevard and green spaces, Alte Oper bridges the city’s historic elegance and contemporary cultural scene. Whether attended for a canonical symphony or a modern crossover performance, a visit to Alte Oper offers insight into Frankfurt’s resilience, civic identity and enduring commitment to the arts.

Walking Beside Rhein

Walking along the banks of the River Rhein in Frankfurt am Main offers a serene escape into nature amid the urban landscape. The well-maintained riverside promenades provide picturesque views of the water, dotted with boats and framed by lush greenery. As you stroll, you can enjoy the gentle sounds of flowing water and the sight of swans gliding gracefully. The pathways are lined with benches, perfect for pausing to take in the scenery or watch the sunset over the city skyline. Along the way, you'll find charming cafés and beer gardens where you can savor local delicacies while enjoying panoramic river views. The area is also popular for jogging, cycling, and picnicking, making it a vibrant spot for both relaxation and recreation. Seasonal events and festivals often take place along the riverbanks, adding to the lively atmosphere. Walking beside the Rhein not only provides a refreshing break from city life but also connects you to Frankfurt's natural beauty and tranquil ambiance.
